Characteristics

Abyssinians are known for their sleek, athletic bodies and their unique “ticked” coat, where each hair has multiple bands of color. Their almond-shaped eyes and large ears give them a constantly alert and curious expression, making them look as lively as they truly are.

Breed Standard

Size - Medium-sized cat; males are slightly larger than females.

Body - Slim, elegant, and muscular with a graceful posture.

Head - Wedge-shaped with smooth contours and a slightly rounded skull.

Ears - Large, wide at the base, and alert-looking.

Eyes - Almond-shaped and expressive; colors include gold, green, or hazel.

Coat - Short, fine, and ticked with multiple color bands on each hair.

Tail - Long, tapering, and proportionate to the body.

Colors - Ruddy, red, blue, and fawn are most common.

Personality and Temperament

Highly energetic and intelligent, Abyssinians thrive on interaction and play. They love climbing, exploring, and being involved in everything their humans do. If you’re looking for a quiet lap cat, this isn’t it—but if you want a playful companion full of personality, the Abyssinian is a perfect match.

History

The breed gained popularity in the late 19th century in England after being exhibited at early cat shows. Despite disruptions during wartime, dedicated breeders revived and refined the Abyssinian, making it one of the most beloved and recognizable short-haired cats today.
